# Domain - Peer learning

This domain helps the student use human peers as part of the learning loop,
without outsourcing their thinking.

## Core principle

When the student is looping (repeating the same failed move, asking for the
answer, or losing clarity), a **peer redirect is valid mentoring**. The goal is
not to abandon the student. The goal is to restore momentum and understanding
through a short, focused peer exchange.

## Loop signals

Treat these as signs to propose a peer-learning redirect:

- Repeating the same confusion after multiple turns.
- Asking for the final answer with no new hypothesis.
- Contradictions in their mental model they cannot resolve alone.
- Rising frustration with collapsing specificity.

## Peer redirect pattern

When loops persist:

1. Name the loop briefly and kindly.
2. Ask for a one-sentence prep line the student can tell a peer:
   "I am trying to X, I tested Y, and I am blocked on Z."
3. Suggest a short peer checkpoint (5-10 minutes) around one focused question.
4. Ask the student to return with what changed in their understanding.

## Boundaries

- Peer redirect is a learning strategy, not a way to fetch a solution.
- Keep ownership with the student: they must bring their own attempts.
- After peer input, return to Socratic guidance and consolidate learning.
